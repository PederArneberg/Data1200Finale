From Pupil to Student

There are certain challenges in the transition from being a pupil to becoming a student. One of the biggest changes is the amount of mandatory classes. In high school the pupils often have mandatory classes from 08:00 until 15:30, whereas students have some weekly classes that they can choose to attend to or not. This freedom can be difficult for new students to manage and the responsibility can be difficult to handle. The lack of homework that the pupils are used too, can also be a difficulty in the transition. Homework are great for creating a steady and evenly workload, the lack of homework in combination with poor self discipline can cause that the student waits until the last minute to get their work done. For some students this method works, but it could lead to a feeling of stress and absence of control, and the fear of not getting through the syllabus in time for the exam.

Another challenge in the transition from being a pupil to a student, is the difference in how the student is graded. The pupils usually have several test, a mid-year test and possibly a final exam. The grading for students is often determined by the school, faculty and the study they attend. In Norway it’s not unusual for the student to have 1-3 mandatory assignments and then a final exam in one course throughout the semester. Usually the mandatory assignments do not count towards the final grade, they just need a pass in order to take the final exam. And then the final exam will determine the students grade.

A Social Challenge

A last challenge is the social interactions with other pupils/students and getting friends at school. In high school most people get friends because they are in the same class, or were friends before they startet high school. Because of the everyday «small» classes, with the same group and the high levels of interactions, pupils have good terms for making friends. As a student it could be more challenging to make friends because its often not a «classroom» environment, instead it could be a lecture hall with 100-400 students who all sits quiet in their chair and listens to a professor, taking notes. Therefore the students need to put a lot more effort into the process of getting friends than the pupils. However the universities and other higher educational institutions often offer a broad range of activities or social groups that the student can be a member of.

Overall the transition from being a pupil to a student is big step, and has different challenges. The students has a lot more responsibility regarding attending classes, managing their workload and getting friends. These are all some of the challenges that the group members have experienced in their transition from being a pupil to a student.
